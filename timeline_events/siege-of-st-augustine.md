notes: As part of the wider Anglo-Spanish conflict, Governor James Oglethorpe of
  Georgia attacked the Spanish town and fort of St Augustine in modern Florida.
  While the siege was maintained for over a month and Oglethorpe took many
  outlying settlements—including the free Black community at Fort Mose—he was
  eventually forced to withdraw.
